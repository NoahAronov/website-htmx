ABOUT
Student
Noah is a graduate student attending a nonprofit university that is designated a National Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense (CAE-CD) by the National Security Agency (NSA) and the Department of Homeland Security (DHS). While attending he received a scholarship and an excellence award and was nominated to a national leadership honor society by program supervisors.
As a university student he is studying for academic degrees in cybersecurity and computer science, a graduate level exam in electrical and computer engineering, and extra academic courses in signals and communications, computational mathematics, and computational physics.
Outside of these studies Noah has audited university courses in engineering, philosophy, politics, economics, history, social theory, and international relations delivered by Yale, Stanford, and MIT. He was also instructed in basic Russian and Chinese language and culture by the Defense Language Institute Foreign Language Center (DLIFLC).
Engineer
Noah is an engineer with experience in implementing, troubleshooting, and securing users, devices, data, servers, operating systems, hypervisors, VMs and containers, networks, firewalls, development pipelines, applications, web sites, and cloud environments. He has built projects, completed interactive labs, and studied developer documentation for languages including Python, JavaScript, Rust, Go, C#, and Elixir. He has worked with data and web technologies using languages including SQL, KQL, HTML, CSS, JSON, YAML, TOML, XML, and Lua. He has also worked with scripting languages including Bash and PowerShell.
He has experience in network security engineering, cloud security engineering, DevSecOps, and application security engineering. He has security assessment or penetration testing experience in networks, wireless, Active Directory, web/app/API/data, multi-cloud, large language models (LLMs), and aerocraft/satellites. To keep up with modern tradecraft he continually watches security presentations and reads articles by offensive security professionals. He has offensive security experience in open source intelligence (OSINT), operations security (OPSEC), cyber threat intelligence (CTI), and social engineering.
Noah is training and developing experience in red team infrastructure and development, stealth and evasion, adversary emulation/simulation, physical security, and cyberpsychology. He is also training and developing understanding of low-level programming, computer hardware, and electromagnetic signals for research and practice in wireless, side-channel, and physical layer attacks. He is currently working as an Offensive Security Engineer intern and is studying for certifications including Certified Red Team Professional (CRTP) and Certified Red Team Operator (CRTO).
