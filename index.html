About
SUMMARY
Noah is a security engineer with diverse technological knowledge and experience in leadership and customer service. He has developed an interdisciplinary perspective through study and practice in numerous subfields of information technology and information security. He has also specialized in offensive security engineering through study and practice with adversarial tradecraft in practical lab and business settings. He has learned and applied skills in multiple domains of penetration testing/ethical hacking including: network, Linux, Windows, Active Directory, wireless, web/app, API, multi-cloud, LLM, and aerospace/satellite. Noah has seen that a broad understanding of information technology and information security combined with experience of many types of penetration testing techniques across different domains is vital to a well-rounded offensive security engineering professional. As a security researcher he is interested in advanced cyber exploits like supply-chain attacks and side-channel attacks.
ACADEMICS
Noah is a university student at a National Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense (CAE-CD) studying for a Master of Science, Cybersecurity and Information Assurance degree. He also holds a Bachelor of Science, Cybersecurity and Information Assurance degree. During university studies he has obtained over a dozen industry-recognized certifications and has completed over three hundred hands-on practical labs in information technology and information security. Noah has been a volunteer and member with the university cybersecurity club providing graphic design and technical documentation for club events. He has also participated in numerous competitions and capture-the-flag events such as the NSA Codebreaker Challenge, Microsoft cloud and AI skills challenges, and Lakera LLM prompt injection challenges.
